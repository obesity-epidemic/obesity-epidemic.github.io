America's
Obesity Epidemic

Kevin Buhrer, Jonny Moon, Thomas Musser
"Obesity has reached epidemic proportions globally"
- World Health Organization (WHO)
Obesity in the United States has increased at an alarming rate. Currently, more than 2 in 3 adults are considered overweight or obese. Obesity can also lead to a myriad of health issues such as Type 2 diabetes, coronary heart disease, cancer and other non-communicable diseases which cost the US $147 billion in 2008. It is “associated with poorer mental health outcomes, reduced quality of life, and the leading causes of death in the U.S.”

Weight management is an issue that we all have to deal with, and a majority of us in the United States could be doing a better job. Chances are, you yourself could lose a few pounds to be at a “normal weight.”

The goal of this website is to raise awareness of this serious issue that affects people of all ages and demographics. We want to create a place where people can clearly see how the prevalence of obesity has changed over time, and what other factors are strongly associated with it.

The hope is that our project will reveal insightful and meaningful information that will lead to change for individuals and possibly for new policy changes in organizations.
